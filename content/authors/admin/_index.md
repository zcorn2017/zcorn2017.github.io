Yuming Zhou is a PhD student in Plant Biology at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, working under the supervision of Prof. Andrew Leakey and Prof. Diwakar Shukla. His research focuses on computational protein-DNA interactions and DNA large language models. He holds a BSc (Hons) in Bioinformatics with First Class Honors from The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen.

Yuming has extensive research experience in bioinformatics and computational biology, including work on drug-target interaction prediction (SCOPE), microRNA TSS identification (miRStart 2.0), and DNA language model development. His work has been published in top journals including Nature Communications, Nucleic Acids Research, and Translational Andrology and Urology.

Beyond research, Yuming is passionate about education and mentoring. He has supervised and advised multiple iGEM teams, earning numerous Gold Medals. He also has experience teaching organic chemistry, computer science, and bioinformatics courses.
